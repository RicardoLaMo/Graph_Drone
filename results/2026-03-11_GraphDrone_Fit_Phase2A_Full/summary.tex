\section{GraphDrone Fit Phase II-A Full Portfolio}

\paragraph{Benchmark contract.}
Phase II-A evaluates the package-level \texttt{GraphDrone.fit()} architecture on the registered OpenML regression portfolio with public benchmark rows restricted to
\texttt{GraphDrone}, \texttt{TabPFN}, \texttt{TabR}, and \texttt{TabM}.

\paragraph{Portfolio result.}
On the 9-dataset, 3-fold benchmark, GraphDrone wins 4 datasets and TabPFN wins 5:
\[
\texttt{GraphDrone}: 4/9,
\qquad
\texttt{TabPFN}: 5/9.
\]
GraphDrone remains stronger than both TabR and TabM on all nine datasets.

\paragraph{GraphDrone wins.}
GraphDrone is best on:
\begin{itemize}
\item California Housing OpenML
\item Houses
\item Healthcare Insurance Expenses
\item Concrete Compressive Strength
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Internal geometry signal.}
The best geometry expert beats the anchor in
\[
14 / 27
\]
folds overall, including all folds on:
\begin{itemize}
\item Concrete Compressive Strength
\item Healthcare Insurance Expenses
\item Miami Housing
\end{itemize}
This confirms that geometry specialists are real contributors, but not yet harvested consistently enough to dominate the public GraphDrone result.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
Phase II-A is a successful architecture checkpoint, not an endpoint.
The remaining gap is an integration problem:
specialist quality exists, but the routing/integration layer still leaves part of that gain on the table.
